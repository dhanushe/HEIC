What is an abstract?

An abstract is a short summary of an in-depth analysis on a research topic.
The abstract accurately states the outcomes of your project and main objectives that your project hits.
An abstract should be complete, concise, clear, and cohesive.

Contents of a Abstract

- The purpose of the project identifying the area of study to which it belongs -
- The methods used to address this research problem, documents or evidence analyzed -
- What is new to our understanding as the result of your inquiry? -
- The significance of the research project. Why are the results useful? -

What are the judges looking for?

The judge will score your application based on these five principles,
examining if your presentation accurately displays the output and impact of your product.
The impact your project has a substantial impact on your scoring.
The judge will consider questions such as:
How impactful was your project on our society?
Did your project improve humankind?
Quality is far more significant than quantity. If your project has a substantial impact but less quality,
understanding will be harder for the judge and not look appealing.
Presenting it in the finest quality is critical to creating the foremost appeal.
How accurate is your project?
Can it be developed into an actual product?
Does it efficiently produce an output?
